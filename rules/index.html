HACKATHON RULES



Hacking starts

May 31st 2016., 10:00 AM

Hacking ends

June 1st 2016, 10:00 AM

There will be

Pizza, snacks, refreshments, energy bars, water, beer, caffein drinks, WiFi, power, and some resting areas.

Bring your own

Laptops, mobiles, tablets. You are allowed to use your own code libs for UI/UX or other common operations.

Team Size

Teams of up to 5 humans are allowed.

Team profiles

Teams can be formed of students, company teams, or freelance hackers. Or you can mix.

What to Build

The Shift Hackathon will be open themed, meaning you can build any app you like.

Publishing code

Since you can build any app, you can be prepared for it beforehand, but all coding must be done during the hackathon. You are required to use GIT and publish your code to the Shift Hackathon repository so it can be easily reviewed. Don’t forget to commit your changes (at least few times in an hour).

If we suspect your app could not be finished in 24 hours, your code will be reviewed and analyzed in more detail, and you may be asked to explain how it could be done in that period of time.

Use your own hacks

Feel free to use any tools, tricks, shortcuts, or workarounds you’ve discovered over the years to help you build quicker. Use any frameworks, hosting service you please.

Ownership and Intellectual Property

Teams have full ownership of everything they build at Codeanywhere Shift Hackathon. You are free to delete your repository after the conference has ended.

Submissions

Each team will have to provide runnable unit at the end of 24 hours hack cycle. Incomplete non-runnable solutions will be disqualified.

Who wins the Shift Prizes?

A team with the completed stable solution and demonstrates it in the best manner, according to judges. Creative and innovative solutions and cool extra features are graded more!

Who Wins the Sponsor Prizes?

Each sponsor will be at the Hackathon and present what is needed to win their prize. The sponsors will decide who wins their prize and is not in the domain of the Hackathon organisation.

Criteria to be eligible for IBM credits: Is my startup qualified to join?

This program is designed for startup businesses. As such, to be eligible to participate, an applicant:

Must have been founded (if the applicant is a corporation, must have been incorporated) within the 5 years immediately preceding the application;
Must have less than $1,000,000 USD in revenues during the 12 months immediately preceding the application;
Must not have been a paying customer of any of the covered IBM Cloud services prior to the application;
To be eligible for participation at the US$ 10,000 per month level, must be working with an approved entrepreneurial community partner (such as a VC, accelerator, or incubator). Startups can contact their community partner to find out more;
Startup cannot be a prior Bluemix customer and use allocated cloud credit towards Bluemix. Similarly, startup cannot be a prior SoftLayer customer and use allocated cloud credit towards SoftLayer;
IBM reserves the right to prioritize based on additional criteria.

Presenting

You will be presenting at least once...hopefully twice. Each team after they have submitted their app at 10:00 AM on the 1st of June will have until 17:00PM on the 1st of June to rest and prepare 90 second presentation of their app in front of mentors and Judges.

The best 16 teams will get to pitch onstage at the Codeanywhere Shift Conference on the 2nd or 3rd of June to win the prizes.

Cheaters

Will be disqualified. Cheating is not cool.

Go hack!

It’s a hackathon! Use whatever platforms, languages or hacks you have in your arsenal, and have fun!
